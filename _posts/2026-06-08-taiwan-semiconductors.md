The Taiwanese chip industry shows how technology, geopolitics, economic security, and modern diplomacy are increasingly interconnected.

Taiwan’s importance in the Indo-Pacific region continues to grow through semiconductor supply chains, U.S.–China strategic competition, and rising international investments in technological resilience.

Countries like India are also increasing their focus on semiconductor manufacturing and strategic technological partnerships through initiatives such as the India Semiconductor Mission (ISM).

As a Political Science student interested in cybersecurity and Open Source Intelligence (OSINT), I find it fascinating how emerging technologies are shaping diplomacy, national security, and strategic affairs in the modern world.
